Use Cases

Decision intelligence helps warehouses avoid costly mistakes at critical moments—when equipment needs replacing, costs need cutting, waste needs managing, or operations need recovering.

Pallet truck selection

Buy vs rent vs used

Situation

A warehouse needs to replace an ageing electric pallet truck. The operations manager is under pressure to get a replacement quickly and has received quotes from two suppliers.

Common mistake

Accepting the first available quote without assessing actual usage patterns. Many warehouses buy new when refurbished would suffice, or purchase outright when short-term rental better fits seasonal demand.

Decision output

Analysis shows usage is concentrated in Q4 peak season. Recommendation: 3-month rental during peak, with option to purchase refurbished unit in Q1 when prices drop and urgency is lower.

Outcome

Avoided £8,000+ immediate outlay. Rental covered peak demand. Refurbished unit purchased later at 40% below new price, with warranty.

Stretch wrap & consumables

Cost reduction

Situation

A fulfilment centre is spending significantly on stretch wrap, labels, and packaging tape. The procurement team believes switching supplier will reduce costs.

Common mistake

Focusing only on unit price rather than usage efficiency. Cheaper wrap often requires more passes to secure loads, increasing actual consumption and labour time.

Decision output

Usage audit reveals over-wrapping on 60% of pallets. Recommendation: upgrade to pre-stretch film (reduces material by 30%), train staff on correct wrap patterns, and standardise pallet configurations.

Outcome

Annual wrap spend reduced by 25% through better technique and material choice—without changing supplier. Additional time savings from faster wrapping process.

Waste-to-value

Wood, metal, cardboard, plastic

Situation

A retail distribution centre generates large volumes of cardboard, broken pallets, shrink wrap, and occasional scrap metal. Current approach: pay for general waste collection.

Common mistake

Treating all waste as a cost rather than segmenting by material value. Mixed waste incurs disposal fees, while separated streams can generate revenue or reduce collection costs.

Decision output

Waste stream analysis identifies opportunities: bale cardboard for collection rebate, segregate LDPE wrap for recycling, repair pallets in-house where viable, and sell scrap metal quarterly.

Outcome

Waste costs converted to net-neutral position. Cardboard rebates offset remaining disposal fees. Pallet repair reduced replacement purchases by 20%.

Disruption recovery

Equipment failure / temporary shutdown

Situation

A 3PL warehouse experiences a conveyor system failure during a busy period. Operations are partially halted, and the client is escalating. The pressure is to fix or replace immediately.

Common mistake

Panic-buying replacement equipment or accepting the first repair quote. Under pressure, warehouses often overspend on expedited solutions that don't address root causes or long-term needs.

Decision output

Continuity assessment prioritises actions: (1) temporary manual workaround for critical lanes, (2) diagnostic to confirm repair vs replace, (3) rental equipment if repair exceeds 5 days, (4) root cause review post-recovery.

Outcome

Operations resumed within 48 hours using temporary measures. Repair completed at 30% of replacement cost. Root cause addressed to prevent recurrence.
