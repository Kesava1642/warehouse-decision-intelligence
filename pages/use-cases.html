Pallet Truck Selection

Buy vs Rent vs Used — Equipment Decision

Illustrative (until pilot confirmed)

Context

A medium-sized warehouse (15,000 sq ft) needs to replace an ageing electric pallet truck. The operations manager is under pressure to get a replacement quickly and has received quotes from two suppliers. Budget is constrained, but operations cannot continue without MHE coverage.

Decision to Make

  • Buy new equipment outright
  • Lease/finance new equipment
  • Purchase refurbished/used unit
  • Short-term rental during peak
  • Delay and repair existing unit

Inputs Required

  • Current usage hours per week
  • Seasonal demand patterns (peak vs baseline)
  • Available budget (capex vs opex)
  • Expected lifespan requirement
  • Maintenance/service capability on-site
  • Urgency level (days to critical)

Method

Scoring factors:

  • Total cost of ownership (3-year view)
  • Cash flow impact timing
  • Operational fit (usage alignment)
  • Risk exposure (warranty, reliability)

Constraints applied:

  • Max capex £12,000
  • Must be operational within 14 days
  • Minimum 2-year expected useful life

Risks weighted:

  • Overspend on underutilised asset
  • Reliability issues with used equipment
  • Lock-in with long-term lease

Output (Deliverable)

Recommendation: 3-month rental during Q4 peak, with option to purchase refurbished unit in Q1 when prices typically drop and urgency is lower.

Report includes:

  • Decision matrix comparing all 5 options
  • Cost breakdown (upfront, monthly, 3-year total)
  • Supplier shortlist (2-3 options per route)
  • Risk mitigation actions
  • Implementation timeline

Expected Impact Metrics

Illustrative / Expected outcomes:

  • Avoid £8,000+ immediate outlay
  • Rental covers peak demand at £400/month
  • Refurbished purchase at 40% below new price
  • 12-month warranty included on refurb unit
  • Payback period: 18 months vs 36 months (new)

Caveats & Assumptions

  • Usage data based on client-provided estimates
  • Pricing reflects current UK market rates
  • Assumes standard warehouse floor conditions
  • Tax treatment depends on business structure
  • Supplier availability may vary by region

Consumables Cost Reduction

Stretch Wrap & Packaging — Efficiency Decision

Illustrative (until pilot confirmed)

Context

A fulfilment centre (50+ staff, 500 pallets/week) is spending £2,800/month on stretch wrap, labels, and packaging tape. The procurement team believes switching supplier will reduce costs, but has no usage baseline.

Decision to Make

  • Switch to cheaper supplier (same spec)
  • Upgrade to pre-stretch film (less material)
  • Invest in powered pre-stretch wrapper
  • Train staff on wrapping technique
  • Standardise pallet configurations

Inputs Required

  • Current monthly spend breakdown
  • Pallets wrapped per week (by type)
  • Current wrap spec (gauge, width)
  • Labour time per pallet
  • Load damage/return rate
  • Storage and handling constraints

Method

Scoring factors:

  • Material cost per pallet
  • Labour time per pallet
  • Load security rating
  • Implementation effort

Constraints applied:

  • No capital investment over £500
  • Must maintain load security standards
  • Changes implementable within 30 days

Risks weighted:

  • Load damage from under-wrapping
  • Staff resistance to technique change
  • Supplier switching costs (min orders)

Output (Deliverable)

Recommendation: Upgrade to pre-stretch film + implement standardised wrap patterns. No supplier change needed. Training investment pays back within 8 weeks.

Report includes:

  • Usage audit findings (over-wrap identified on 60% of pallets)
  • Cost comparison: current vs optimised
  • Wrap specification recommendations
  • Training plan outline
  • Monitoring metrics for ongoing savings

Expected Impact Metrics

Illustrative / Expected outcomes:

  • 25% reduction in annual wrap spend
  • £700/month saving (£8,400/year)
  • 30% less material per pallet
  • 15 seconds saved per pallet (labour)
  • No increase in load damage rate

Caveats & Assumptions

  • Savings assume consistent implementation
  • Results vary by pallet mix and load types
  • Training effectiveness depends on staff engagement
  • Pre-stretch film pricing based on bulk rates
  • Labour savings calculated at average wage rates

More Decision Scenarios

Additional areas where structured decision intelligence applies

Waste-to-value

Wood, metal, cardboard, plastic

Situation: Distribution centre paying for general waste collection on mixed materials including cardboard, broken pallets, shrink wrap, and scrap metal.

Decision: Segment waste streams—bale cardboard for rebate, segregate LDPE wrap, repair pallets in-house, sell scrap metal quarterly.

Expected impact: Waste costs converted to net-neutral. Pallet repair reduces replacement purchases.

Disruption recovery

Equipment failure / temporary shutdown

Situation: 3PL warehouse with conveyor system failure during peak period. Operations halted, client escalating, pressure to fix immediately.

Decision: Prioritised continuity actions—temporary manual workaround, diagnostic for repair vs replace, rental option if repair exceeds 5 days.

Expected impact: Operations resume within 48 hours. Repair at 30% of replacement cost. Root cause addressed.
