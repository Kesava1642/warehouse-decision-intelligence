Trust & Neutrality

Our value depends on giving you honest recommendations. Here's how we stay independent, even when suppliers are part of the picture.

Concept-stage service. No live provider onboarding or bookings.

ClarityOps does not provide repair services. All coordination is advisory and decision-support only.

Current status: During the initial pilot phase, ClarityOps does not operate a live provider marketplace. Any supplier names or examples shown are placeholders used solely to demonstrate workflow and decision structure.

How we score decisions

Our recommendations are based on consistent, transparent criteria. We score options against:

  • Cost — Total cost of ownership, not just upfront price
  • Reliability — Track record, warranties, and support
  • Availability — Lead times, stock, and capacity to deliver
  • Risk — Operational and financial risk of each option
  • Compliance — Safety, regulatory, and certification fit
  • ROI — Return and payback where relevant to the decision
  • Continuity — Impact on business continuity and resilience

Independence: Our scoring is not tied to any supplier. We do not accept payment to favour one option over another. Recommendations are formed from your situation and our framework, not from commercial relationships.

Limitations: We provide advice to support your decision. Final responsibility for any purchase, contract, or operational change remains with you as the buyer. We do not act as your agent or assume liability for outcomes.

Data handling: We do not store sensitive business data. The pilot uses email contact only; no confidential operational data is retained. Information you share is used solely to form your recommendation and is not sold or shared for marketing.

Decision-first, products-second

We analyse your situation and form a recommendation before any supplier is involved. The decision comes first. Supplier options come after—and only if the decision points toward a purchase, rental, or service.

This means our recommendation might be "do nothing", "delay", "repair instead of replace", or "change your process rather than buy equipment". These are valid outcomes. If a purchase isn't the right answer, we won't push one.

Supplier neutrality rules

We do not operate a "pay-to-win" model. Suppliers cannot pay to appear higher in recommendations or to be included in results where they don't genuinely fit.

  • Suppliers are matched based on your decided requirements—not on advertising spend
  • We do not accept payments to favour one supplier over another
  • All suppliers shown must meet the specification your decision defines
  • We clearly label any commercial relationships

If no suitable supplier exists in our directory for your requirement, we say so. We don't stretch a match to fill the gap.

Referral relationships

Decisions are formed independently before any supplier is introduced. If you proceed with a supplier through our service, we may receive a referral fee. This is optional and does not affect your recommendation.

How we keep decisions neutral:

  • The decision (buy/rent/used/delay) is finalised before suppliers are shown
  • Suppliers are not ranked or prioritised by referral arrangement
  • Any commercial relationship is disclosed
  • You are always free to go direct to any supplier

Our incentive is to give you a good decision that you act on—not to push a specific supplier.

Data & privacy

We don't need—or want—your confidential employer data. Our assessments are based on operational context, not sensitive business information.

  • We ask about warehouse type, size, usage patterns, and budget ranges
  • We do not require client lists, revenue figures, or proprietary processes
  • Information you share is used only to form your recommendation
  • We do not sell or share your data with third parties for marketing

If you proceed to a supplier introduction, we share only the information needed to fulfil your request—with your knowledge.

Limits of the demo

The decision demo on this site is an MVP—a working prototype to show how the service functions. It has limitations:

  • Supplier names are placeholders, not live suppliers
  • Decision logic covers common scenarios but not every edge case
  • Price bands and lead times shown are indicative, not live quotes
  • The full service includes human review; the demo is automated only

We're building this in public, starting with a UK pilot. The demo shows intent and approach. Any provider onboarding, verification, or booking functionality is planned only after initial pilot delivery.

Trust & Safety

  • We provide independent guidance and are not a product seller.
  • You should always verify any supplier or service provider’s insurance and qualifications before work starts.
  • Health & safety responsibility stays with your warehouse, site rules, and management controls.
  • We cannot guarantee provider availability, response times, or specific outcomes.
