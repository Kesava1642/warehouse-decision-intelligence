Decision Framework

Every warehouse moves through predictable stages. Our framework helps you make the right decisions at each stage—whether you're setting up, running day-to-day, handling problems, or looking for improvements.

The 5-stage warehouse lifecycle

Warehouse operations follow a natural cycle. Understanding where you are helps identify which decisions matter most right now.

1

Start / Setup

New warehouse, new operation, or major expansion. Decisions made here shape costs and capabilities for years.

Typical decisions

  • Racking type and layout configuration
  • Equipment fleet: buy, lease, or rent
  • MHE selection matched to throughput
  • Consumables sourcing and initial stock
  • Safety and compliance setup
2

Operate

Day-to-day running. The focus is on maintaining flow, controlling costs, and catching problems early.

Typical decisions

  • Equipment maintenance vs replacement
  • Consumables reorder quantities and suppliers
  • Waste handling and disposal routines
  • Seasonal capacity adjustments
  • Staff training and process updates
3

Disruption

Something goes wrong. Equipment fails, suppliers let you down, or demand spikes unexpectedly. Fast decisions are needed.

Typical decisions

  • Repair vs replace under time pressure
  • Temporary equipment hire
  • Alternative supplier activation
  • Process workarounds to maintain output
  • Client communication and priority triage
4

Recovery / Continuity

Getting back to normal after disruption. The goal is stable operations and preventing the same problem recurring.

Typical decisions

  • Permanent fix vs continued workaround
  • Root cause analysis and prevention
  • Insurance and supplier claims
  • Backup equipment or supplier arrangements
  • Updated continuity procedures
5

Optimise

Operations are stable. Now is the time to find efficiencies, reduce costs, and prepare for growth or change.

Typical decisions

  • Equipment upgrades for efficiency
  • Consumables renegotiation or consolidation
  • Waste-to-value opportunities
  • Layout and process improvements
  • Capacity planning for future growth
